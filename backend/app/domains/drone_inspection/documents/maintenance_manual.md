# Inspection Drone Maintenance Manual

## Battery Health and Error E-204

Error E-204 indicates battery voltage sag under load. The usual causes are aged cells,
high wind demand, cold pack temperature, or aggressive climb commands. When E-204 appears
with battery health below 75 percent, the operator should end the mission, return to
launch, and schedule a battery internal-resistance check.

## ESC Temperature Warning

ESC-TEMP-WARN means an electronic speed controller exceeded the sustained inspection
temperature threshold. Continue only if the aircraft is close to launch and vibration
is normal. If ESC temperature is above 80 C or motor vibration exceeds 5 mm/s, stop the
mission and inspect cooling airflow, propeller damage, and motor bearings.

## Maintenance Ticket Evidence

Every maintenance ticket should include system id, event codes, battery health, state of
charge, ESC temperature, vibration, wind speed, and the latest operator question.
